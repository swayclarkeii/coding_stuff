## Key Decision Points

### Decision 1: Which Project First?
**Timeline:** Late January 2026 (after follow-up calls)
**Options:**
1. **Ambushed first** (recommended) - clear ROI, unlocks time for Bold Move TV
2. **Bold Move TV first** - lower complexity, faster win, but smaller impact
3. **Parallel development** - higher cost, faster overall delivery, resource intensive

**Recommendation:** Ambushed first
**Rationale:**
- ‚Ç¨21K-27K annual value vs unknown ROI (Bold Move TV)
- Time savings directly enable Bold Move TV sales focus
- Admin team buy-in creates foundation for future automations
- Budget available (Ambushed pays the bills)

### Decision 2: How Much Automation?
**Timeline:** During proposal phase
**Options:**
1. **Validation only** - AI flags errors, Sindbad reviews, manual payments
2. **Validation + payment prep** - AI prepares Wise batch, Sindbad approves and executes
3. **Full automation** - AI validates, prepares, and executes payments (Sindbad audit trail)

**Recommendation:** Start with #2, scale to #3
**Rationale:**
- Build trust incrementally
- Maintain human checkpoint during pilot
- Reduce risk of payment errors
- Gradual adoption path

### Decision 3: CRM for Bold Move TV?
**Timeline:** During Bold Move TV strategy call
**Options:**
1. **Upgrade Google Sheets** - familiar, low cost, limited features
2. **HubSpot Free** - robust, CRM designed, learning curve
3. **Pipedrive** - sales-focused, similar to Ambushed (if Eugene connection), paid
4. **Notion** - flexible, visual, multi-purpose

**Recommendation:** Pending partner preferences and budget
**Rationale:**
- Partner must use it daily (adoption critical)
- Budget constraints (Bold Move TV not yet profitable)
- Integration complexity varies by tool
- Don't over-engineer for current scale
