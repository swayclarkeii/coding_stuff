## Feedback Analysis

- **Humility:** Self-deprecating about sales skills, partnership mistakes
- **Enthusiasm:** Genuine excitement when seeing solutions (Descript reaction)
- **Pragmatism:** Focused on practical outcomes over theoretical ideals

### Decision-Making Style
- **Collaborative:** Wants input from team and partner
- **Reflective:** Takes time to consider implications (strategy uncertainty)
- **Quality-focused:** Prefers doing things right over doing them fast
- **ROI-conscious:** Clear about time value and business impact

### Trust Indicators
- Shared confidential information about partnership issues
- Openly discussed budget constraints
- Accepted recommendations immediately (Descript, stakeholder inclusion)
- Committed to follow-up calls without hesitation

### Potential Friction Points
- May be reluctant to give up control (wants oversight)

## Recommendations for Future Interactions

### Communication Approach
1. **Be direct but consultative:** Client appreciates honesty and expertise
2. **Include rationale:** Explain why, not just what
3. **Respect time:** Client had hard stop, values efficiency
4. **Acknowledge constraints:** Work within budget and time realities

### Solution Design Principles
1. **Control points:** Always include human approval checkpoints
2. **Whole team benefit:** Consider admin team pain points, not just founder's
3. **Tactful automation:** Avoid "spammy" or aggressive approaches
4. **Clear ROI:** Quantify time savings and revenue impact

### Engagement Strategy
1. **Discovery depth:** Client is open to thorough discovery process
2. **Stakeholder inclusion:** Bring in relevant team members early
3. **Phased delivery:** Separate Ambushed and Bold Move TV clearly
4. **Strategy consultation:** Some strategy work may be needed/valued alongside automation
