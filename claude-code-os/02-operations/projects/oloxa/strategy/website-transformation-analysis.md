## Case Study Framework (From Transcript)

### Template: Surface Request → Real Problem → Solution → Impact

**Example 1: Professional Services Firm**
- **Surface Request:** "Automate our proposal process" (technical ask)
- **Diagnostic Discovery:** "Why do proposals take so long?" (diagnostic question)
- **Real Problem:** Requirements gathering, not proposal creation (root cause)
- **Solution:** Redesigned discovery process with structured intake, not proposal automation (strategic)
- **Impact:** 70% time reduction + 30% close rate increase = $50K+ annual impact

**Example 2: Manufacturing Company**
- **Surface Request:** "Automate inventory tracking"
- **Diagnostic Discovery:** "What causes inventory issues?"
- **Real Problem:** No clear reorder protocols, inconsistent purchasing decisions
- **Solution:** Established reorder points + purchasing authority + visibility dashboards
- **Impact:** From monthly inventory issues to quarterly + $100K+ annual impact

**Example 3: Accounting Firm**
- **Surface Request:** "Automate client update emails"
- **Diagnostic Discovery:** "Why are clients dissatisfied with updates?"
- **Real Problem:** Generic updates, not communication quality
- **Solution:** Redesigned communication framework + quality templates
- **Impact:** Improved client satisfaction scores + reduced communication issues

## Messaging Patterns to Adopt

### 1. Diagnostic Language (Coach Background)

**Pattern:** Ask questions that uncover root causes

- "What's really happening here?"
- "The problem you describe is rarely the actual problem."
- "Let me ask you a different question..."
- "Most business owners see chaos but can't pinpoint what's broken."
- "You know things could be better, but can't identify where to start."

### 2. Partnership Language (Not Vendor)

**Pattern:** Collaborative discovery, not transaction delivery

- "Let's discover what's actually broken"
- "We'll work together to redesign your process"
- "I'll guide you through transformation"
- NOT: "We'll build you X"
- NOT: "Our services include..."

### 3. Business Outcome Language (Not Technical Features)

**Pattern:** Lead with impact, not implementation

- ❌ NOT: "I'll build you a multi-step automation with advanced filtering and conditional logic"
- ✅ INSTEAD: "I'll help you ensure high-priority leads get immediate attention while your sales team focuses on prospects most likely to buy - creating $50K+ in annual impact."

### 4. Human/Story Language (Authenticity)

**Pattern:** Personal anecdotes, vulnerable moments, relatable pain

- "I learned..."
- "In my experience..."
- "I've been there..."
- "For years, I helped parents transform their relationships by asking better questions. When I became an entrepreneur, I realized businesses face the same challenge."

---

## Language to AVOID

### ❌ Red Flags - Do Not Use

1. **Technical Jargon** (unless explaining implementation AFTER diagnosis)
   - n8n, Zapier, API, webhooks, integrations, workflows
   - Exception: Brief mention in "how I implement" section, never in headlines

2. **Feature Lists Without Outcomes**
   - "24/7 voice and chat assistants" → Instead: "Never miss a customer inquiry, even at 3am"
   - "Automate invoicing and emails" → Instead: "Eliminate the administrative chaos preventing you from focusing on growth"

3. **Automation Expert Positioning**
   - "AI automation expert"
   - "n8n specialist"
   - "Workflow builder"
   - Instead: "AI Transformation Partner," "Business Problem Diagnostician"

4. **Vendor-Client Transactional Language**
   - "Our services include..."
   - "We offer..."
   - "Get a quote"
   - Instead: "Let's discover together," "I'll guide you," "Start with a discovery session"

5. **Generic Agency-Speak**
   - "Cutting-edge AI solutions"
   - "Innovative automation"
   - "Transform your business" (without specifics)
   - Instead: Specific problems, specific outcomes, specific transformation stories

## Recommended Balance

### The 80/20 Rule

**80% of copy:** Business problems, outcomes, personal story
- Pain points and frustrations
- Diagnostic questions
- Case study transformations
- Personal background and credibility
- Business outcomes and impact

**20% of copy:** Technical capabilities (as enabling tools)
- How you implement (after diagnosis)
- Tools used (briefly mentioned)
- Technical process (simplified)

**Always lead with WHY (business problem) before HOW (technical solution)**
