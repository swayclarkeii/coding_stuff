## Day Structure

### Morning (9:00 - 11:00)
- Opens Google Sheets calendar to see all active projects
- Goes project-by-project, top to bottom
- Checks overnight emails (many US-based clients)
- Digests briefs, gives direction to team members
- Ensures everyone knows what they're doing
- Handles handovers when freelancers switch
- "Onslaught" of messages to respond to

### Afternoon (11:00 - 18:00)
- Lower tempo, calmer period
- Does long-form work: visual research, AI image generation
- Handles meetings (like this interview)
- Prepares for potential evening issues
- Works on mood boards for tomorrow's projects
- Anticipates sticking points before they become problems

### Evening (18:00 - 02:00)
- Second "onslaught" begins ~6 PM
- Reviews work from freelancers ("Hey James, check this")
- Gives seal of approval, comments, critiques
- Sometimes gets hands-on to tweak designs
- Prepares tomorrow's workload
- Creates mood boards for projects starting next day

## Pain Points Identified

### 1. Extreme Work Hours (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Description:** Working 9AM - 2AM on work days
**Impact:** Burnout risk, no time for strategic work
**Quantification:** ~17 hour days × 4 days = 68 hours/week
**Quote:** "That's the one thing I really want to change"

### 2. Project Management Overhead (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Description:** Constant message answering, blocking deep work
**Impact:** Can't focus on mood boards and art direction
**Quantification:** ~1/3 of day = 5+ hours/day
**Quote:** "Painting the Golden Gate Bridge - by the time you finish responding, there's more messages"
**Components:**
- Morning onslaught: 1.5-2 hours
- Evening onslaught: 3 hours (6PM-9PM)
- Scattered throughout day

### 3. Mood Board Bottleneck (CRITICAL)
**Description:** Must create mood boards before projects start or quality suffers
**Impact:** If not done, designers "freestyle" → lower hit rate → more rework for future James/Zoe
**Trigger:** Needs to know platform (Figma vs ReadyMag vs InDesign) before can start
**Quote:** "If I don't do a mood board... the success rate is going to be lower, and it's just going to make more work for future me"

### 4. Freelancer Anxiety / Bottleneck Stress (MEDIUM)
**Description:** Worries freelancers are waiting on him, feels he can't take breaks
**Impact:** Personal stress, can't step away
**Quote:** "I get really stressed that people are like waiting on me"
**Magic Wand Wish:** Some kind of queue system so people know where they stand

### 5. AI Tool Chaos (MEDIUM)
**Description:** No oversight on who's using what, who's paying for what
**Impact:** Unknown costs, inconsistent tool usage, redundant subscriptions
**Details:**
- Everyone was offered Midjourney package → now redundant
- James pays for Reeve, shares with everyone
- Unknown who has personal AI subscriptions
- CREA/Nanobanana costs unclear ("real cash sink")
**Quote:** "I don't know who's paying for what, if they're paying out of their own pocket or invoicing us"

### 6. Document Management Chaos (MEDIUM-LOW but compound)
**Description:** Project documents scattered across OPS email threads
**Impact:** Freelancers can't find latest versions, ask James
**Pain Rating:** 3/10 individually, but "death by 1000 papercuts"
**Quote:** "Hey James, can you tell me if this is the latest text? I get that question loads."
**Components:**
- Multiple text document versions (Word, pasted in email, Google Docs)
- No single source of truth per project
- WeTransfer links expire
- Freelancers coming in late miss context

### 7. Internal Projects Stalled (LOW - frustration)
**Description:** Strategic initiatives never get done (website update, newsletter, new revenue streams)
**Impact:** Company not evolving
**Quote:** "I could do anything here, but I just don't have the time to do it"
**Blocker:** No clarity on delegating to Madalena, Elise, Leonor

### 8. Platform Uncertainty (LOW)
**Description:** Doesn't always know which design platform (Figma/ReadyMag/InDesign) before project starts
**Impact:** Can't create mood board until known
**Quote:** "One thing that really grinds my gears"

## Process Maps

### Morning Onslaught Process
```
1. Open Google Sheets calendar (project list)
2. Go top to bottom through each project
3. For each project:
   a. Check overnight emails (US clients)
   b. Digest brief/feedback
   c. Open Discord channel for that project
   d. Message team: direction, "good morning", what to focus on
   e. Handle any handovers (new person on project)
   f. Answer questions from freelancers
4. Repeat until all projects covered
5. Duration: ~1.5-2 hours
```

### Mood Board Creation Process
```
1. Receive brief (agency deck, director notes)
2. Confirm platform (Figma/ReadyMag/InDesign) - BLOCKER if unknown
3. Pull references from previous treatments
4. Write notes about what works
5. Sometimes generate AI assets for title pages
6. Create typeface combinations
7. Compile into mood board document
8. Share with designer before project starts
```

### AI Image Request Process
```
1. Brief comes in with impossible visual need
   ("Cinderella holding oranges drinking Coca-Cola")
2. Decision: Can we find existing visual research? Or need AI?
3. If AI needed:
   a. Old process: Generate fresh in Midjourney (chaotic, inconsistent)
   b. New process: Find partial match in existing VR, use Reeve/Nanobanana to edit
4. James, Alexi, or Gaetano handles AI work
5. Regular visual researchers sometimes use Reeve for simple edits
6. AI image goes into visual research batch
7. Sent to director for approval
```

### Evening Review Process
```
1. ~6 PM: Freelancers send "Hey James, check this"
2. Open each submission (design or visual research batch)
3. Review against brief and quality standards
4. Options:
   a. Approve ("seal of approval")
   b. Comment with feedback
   c. Get hands-on and tweak directly
5. If issues found, work with freelancer to fix
6. Repeat for all projects
7. Duration: ~3 hours (6 PM - 9 PM core, extends to 2 AM)
```
